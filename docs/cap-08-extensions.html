Capacity Planning Common Extensions

Typical customizations and how to scope them

Capacity Module Reference

Capacity has fewer extensions than T&Q but the ones that come up tend to add meaningful complexity. Most are driven by Finance wanting more granular cost modeling or Sales wanting more flexible scenario management.

ExtensionSizeNotes
Custom Ramp ProfilesSThe base app provides 3 ramp profiles. Adding more requires a new profile in the admin setup and corresponding formula updates. Common when different geos (APAC vs. EMEA) have materially different ramp times.
Revenue Target by Product LineMWhen Finance provides separate targets for different product lines. Requires adding product dimension to the revenue target and coverage modules — significant dimensionality addition.
OWP IntegrationM–LConnecting Anaplan Workforce Planning data (approved positions, cost data) to the Capacity hiring plan. High value for customers with mature headcount planning processes, but requires both products to be live.
Custom Cost ModelingMAdding fully-loaded cost per hire (base + benefits + equipment + recruiting cost) to the financial liability analysis. Common for CFOs who want full cost-of-hire visibility, not just quota capacity.
Additional Scenario ManagementSThe configurator sets the number of scenarios at generation time. Adding scenarios post-gen requires extending the scenario list and replicating scenario logic across modules. Small effort but needs careful testing.
Segmentation Auto-SyncSAutomating the segment-count feed from Segmentation to Capacity coverage ratios. Currently manual export/import. An ADO pipeline makes this seamless — small setup effort once ADO is configured.
💡 Positioning Tip

The most powerful Capacity demo moment is showing a VP of Sales Finance the "holy grail" page: "Hire 2 AEs in January, 3 in April — here's exactly when you close the revenue gap." Lead with that. Everything else is detail.

Pre-Project Discovery Questions

Use these questions before or during kickoff to assess Capacity Planning fit and identify likely extensions early. ⭐ marks the highest-priority questions.

Role Hierarchy & Headcount

  • ⭐ How many sales roles do you have? Do they map cleanly to a 2–3 level hierarchy (Job Family Group → Job Family → Sales Role)?
  • ⭐ Do different roles have materially different ramp times? Do you have data to support those assumptions, or are they estimates?
  • Do you use role grades (P1–P5 or equivalent)? Do you need grade-level headcount planning, or is role-level sufficient?
  • How do you define "fully ramped"? Is there a consistent definition across the organization, or does it vary by manager?
  • Do you use position management (open headcount positions tied to org structure) or purely people-based planning?

Planning Process

  • ⭐ Who sets the tops-down headcount budget — Finance, Sales Leadership, or both? At what level of geography do they plan (global, region, sub-region)?
  • ⭐ Who enters the bottoms-up hiring plan — territory managers, regional managers, or a central Sales Ops team?
  • Do you currently model revenue contribution by ramp cohort? (If yes, they'll understand the app quickly. If no, plan extra time to explain why it matters.)
  • How many planning scenarios do you need? (Base, conservative, optimistic — or more?)
  • Is your headcount planning currently done in Excel, a legacy tool, or another Anaplan model? What's the biggest pain?

Data & Integration

  • ⭐ Do you currently track actual hire dates vs. planned hire dates? (Required for meaningful AOP snapshot comparison.)
  • Do you use Anaplan Workforce Planning (OWP)? If so, is there appetite to connect headcount data between OWP and Capacity?
  • Do you need cost modeling (salary, benefits, recruiting cost per hire) in addition to headcount counts?
  • Are segment counts available from a Segmentation app, or will coverage ratios be configured manually?
  • How does Finance currently provide revenue targets — by region, by sub-region, by product? At what time granularity?
