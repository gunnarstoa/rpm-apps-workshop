SPM / RPM Overview

The Sales Performance Management landscape and where T&Q fits

Section 1 45 min

What is SPM?

Sales Performance Management (SPM) — also referred to as Revenue Performance Management (RPM) in the Anaplan context — is the discipline of planning, executing, and optimizing how a company structures its sales force to drive revenue. It spans the full arc of sales operations: from designing sales territories and allocating targets, to assigning quotas, planning headcount capacity, and tracking in-year performance against those plans.

Traditionally, these processes are managed in spreadsheets — often a fragmented patchwork of Excel files, shared drives, and manual handoffs between Finance, Sales Ops, and HR. The pain is real and persistent: territory carve-outs done in Excel are brittle, quota roll-ups are error-prone, and there is no single source of truth. When a rep leaves or a territory changes, the cascading updates are nightmare material.

Anaplan's SPM/RPM application suite replaces this chaos with connected, governed, and scalable planning — all on the Anaplan platform. The suite is organized into five distinct pillars, each addressing a specific part of the sales planning lifecycle.

The 5 SPM Pillars

1

Territory & Quota (T&Q)

The cornerstone of the RPM Apps suite. Covers territory design, hierarchy management, target allocation, quota setting, account assignment, and the rule engine. The most mature, most robust app in the portfolio — and the best landing use case for new SPM customers.

⭐ Best Landing App
2

Account Segmentation & Scoring

Helps sales ops define how accounts are scored, tiered, and segmented before territory planning begins. Think of it as the step that feeds clean, classified account data into the territory design process. Useful for customers who segment by firmographics, industry, or potential spend.

Pre-Territory Planning
3

Go-to-Market Capacity Planning

Strategic headcount planning — how many reps do we need next year, in which regions, at what ramp rates? This app sits above T&Q in the planning hierarchy. It answers "how many territories can we cover?" before you design the territories themselves.

Workforce Planning
4

Sales Forecasting

In-year, CRM-connected forecast tracking and management. Pulls pipeline data from Salesforce (or other CRMs), overlays quota attainment, and supports bottom-up/top-down forecast collaboration. Works well as a second engagement after T&Q is live.

In-Year Tracking
5

Incentive Compensation Management (ICM)

Calculation and management of sales rep commissions and incentive plans. ICM is often the single highest-pain SPM problem — no one loves their current comp tool, and the manual processes (Excel, legacy comp software) are notoriously error-prone and time-consuming. The Anaplan ICM app is on the roadmap and coming later this year.

Why ICM Is a Big Deal

  • Comp disputes are one of the top reasons reps leave — accuracy and transparency matter
  • Most companies still run ICM in Excel or a legacy tool (SAP Callidus, Xactly, etc.) — pain is near-universal
  • ICM is downstream of T&Q — quotas set in T&Q feed directly into comp calculations
  • Once T&Q is live and trusted, ICM is the natural next conversation

How to Position It Now

  • Don't promise a date — say "later this year, watch this space"
  • Frame it as the natural completion of the SPM suite: T&Q sets the quota, ICM pays the rep against it
  • Use it to keep the customer engaged post-T&Q go-live: "When ICM ships, you'll be first in line because your quota data is already structured correctly"
  • Split Allocation (partial ICM functionality) may ship earlier — ask the Apps team for the latest roadmap status before customer conversations

Why T&Q First?

When prospecting or scoping SPM work, T&Q should be your default starting point. Here is why:

The Pain Persists Longer

Territory and quota setting happens every year — often multiple times per year as the business changes. Unlike a one-time data migration, this is a recurring, high-stakes process. The pain of doing it in spreadsheets doesn't go away; it compounds. Every re-org, every new hire, every territory carve means another round of Excel-hell.

Spreadsheets Work… Temporarily

Small sales orgs (<50 reps) can usually manage quotas in Excel. But as the sales force grows, the process breaks. Overlapping territories, stale data, no audit trail, no what-if analysis. By the time they come to Anaplan, they are usually already feeling the pain acutely. That's your opening.

Best Landing Spot

T&Q has the clearest ROI story, the most defined scope, and the most proven delivery motion. It's easier to estimate, easier to implement, and faster to show value than a custom-built SPM solution. The configurator dramatically reduces build time — a well-configured T&Q app can be generated in a day.

Gateway to the Full Suite

Once T&Q is live, you have a foothold. Data is structured, the customer trusts Anaplan, and Sales Ops sees value. From there, GTM Capacity Planning, Sales Forecasting, and eventually ICM become natural next steps. T&Q is the on-ramp to the full RPM platform.

Key T&Q Concepts

Before diving into the configurator, make sure these foundational concepts are clear. Confusing territories with quotas, or targets with quotas, is a common source of miscommunication with customers.

Territories: The "Houses"

Think of territories as houses. A house belongs to the company — it has an address, a value, a set of attributes. Reps are assigned to territories (they live in the house), but they don't own the house. When a rep leaves, the territory doesn't disappear — you just assign a new rep to it.

  • Territories are company-owned — they exist independently of any individual rep
  • Hierarchies define the rollup — territories nest into regions, regions into areas, areas into global (the exact structure is configurable)
  • A territory can have multiple reps (overlays, specialists) and a rep can cover multiple territories
  • Account assignment maps accounts (and their revenue) to territories — this is often the hardest data problem

Targets vs. Quotas: Not the Same Thing

This distinction trips up many customers (and sometimes practitioners). Here is the clean separation:

Concept Level Nature Example
Target Territory Static — tied to the territory, not the person "North Bay territory has a $2.4M target"
Quota Person (Resource) Dynamic — follows the rep, adjusts for ramp/split "Sarah's quota is $1.8M (75% ramp, new hire)"

The distinction matters because a single territory may have 0.5 of a senior rep and 0.3 of a junior rep — the territory target is fixed, but the quota each rep carries is calculated based on their assignment percentage, ramp status, and quota composition rules.

The T&Q Planning Flow

At a high level, the T&Q planning process follows this flow:

  1. Finance sets total revenue targets (top-down) → imported into T&Q
  2. Targets are allocated down the territory hierarchy → region → area → territory
  3. Accounts are assigned to territories → account values inform territory sizing
  4. Resources (reps) are assigned to territories → with assignment percentages
  5. Quotas are calculated → based on territory targets, assignment %, and quota composition rules
  6. Approval workflow → Sales leaders review and approve; overrides possible
  7. Quotas are published → downstream to CRM, ICM, or rep dashboards
📝 Note

Not all customers use all steps. Some customers only need target allocation (no quotas). Some skip the approval workflow. The configurator lets you turn on/off most of these capabilities — the key is understanding what the customer actually needs before you start configuring.
