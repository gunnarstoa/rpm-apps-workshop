RPM Apps Tech Enablement Workshop

Territory & Quota · Account Segmentation · Capacity Planning · Sales Forecasting

RPM Apps Suite 4 Modules Workshop

About This Workshop

The RPM Apps Technical Enablement Workshop is a hands-on technical enablement program designed for Anaplan delivery practitioners — Solutions Consultants, Solution Architects, and Technical Consultants — who will be implementing or supporting the Anaplan Revenue Performance Management (RPM) application suite for customers.

This workshop covers four RPM applications across dedicated modules: Territory & Quota (T&Q), Account Segmentation & Scoring, Go-to-Market Capacity Planning, and Sales Forecasting. Each module follows the same structure — functional overview, data requirements, configurator walkthrough, hands-on labs, post-generation steps, model review, application walkthrough, and common extensions.

By the end of this workshop, participants will have hands-on experience with all three configurators, a working understanding of how data flows between the apps, and the skills to confidently lead a customer through an RPM implementation.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

  1. Understand the RPM Apps suite — articulate all five SPM pillars, explain how each app fits into the broader Revenue Performance Management platform, and position the suite in a customer conversation.
  2. Configure all three apps — work through the T&Q, Segmentation, and Capacity configurators with confidence, making informed decisions on each setting and understanding which choices are difficult to reverse.
  3. Execute post-generation steps — apply the post-gen checklist for each app, identify and resolve common issues, and assess model readiness for customer handoff.
  4. Walk a customer through each application — guide a customer through the full UX of T&Q, Segmentation, and Capacity, explaining each workflow in business terms and identifying the key demo moments.
  5. Use CoModeler effectively — generate line item dictionaries, explain formula logic, and answer specific questions about the generated model structure using Anaplan's AI documentation tool.
  6. Identify and scope common extensions — recognize when base app functionality meets a requirement vs. when a custom extension is needed, and scope extensions in a way that protects the upgrade path.
  7. Understand Sales Forecasting — explain what the app does, who uses it, and how it connects to the rest of the RPM suite.

Prerequisites

To get the most out of this workshop, participants should have:

  • Basic Anaplan platform knowledge — familiarity with modules, lists, dashboards, and model building concepts. This is not an Anaplan 101 session.
  • Completed Anaplan Level 1 certification (or equivalent platform experience)
  • Access to an Anaplan workspace — you will need a workspace with at least one model to run the configurator during the hands-on lab. Confirm with your manager ahead of time.
  • Reviewed the T&Q solution brief — available on the internal enablement portal
  • Downloaded the lab dataset files — see the Configurator Exercise section for the CloudWorks data files. Have these ready before the afternoon lab session.
⚠ Important

Do not run the configurator app generation against a customer workspace during this workshop. The exercise uses a dedicated training workspace. Generating a T&Q app creates ~8,000 objects and cannot be easily undone.

How the Apps Work Together

The three RPM apps — Territory & Quota, Account Segmentation, and Capacity Planning — are designed to work together as a suite. Each module in this workshop covers one app, but understanding how data flows between them is essential before you start.

⚠ These connections are not automatic

Data flows between apps require a manual export/import step today. Native app-to-app connections are on the roadmap. Plan for this in your project timelines.

Segmentation → T&Q

Account segments drive territory carving and Rule Engine logic. Export segments from Segmentation, import as an account attribute in T&Q.

Segmentation → Capacity

Account mix changes drive headcount needs. Segment counts feed directly into Capacity coverage ratios.

Capacity → T&Q

TPH (territories per headcount) output from Capacity informs how many territories to design in T&Q.

Recommended Sequence

  1. Segmentation → finalize account segments
  2. Capacity → determine headcount by role/geo
  3. T&Q → design territories informed by both
