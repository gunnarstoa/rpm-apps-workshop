RPM Apps Tech Enablement Workshop

Territory & Quota · Account Segmentation · Capacity Planning · Sales Forecasting

RPM Apps Suite 4 Modules Workshop

About This Workshop

The RPM Apps Technical Enablement Workshop is a hands-on technical enablement program for Anaplan delivery practitioners — Solutions Consultants, Solution Architects, and Technical Consultants — who will be implementing or supporting the Anaplan Revenue Performance Management (RPM) application suite.

This is not a lecture series. It is a structured sequence of demonstrations, discussions, and hands-on labs designed to take you from knowing about these applications to being capable of configuring, explaining, and delivering them for real customers.

How Each Module Flows

The workshop is organized into four application modules — one per RPM app. Every module follows the same deliberate sequence. The order matters: each section builds on the one before it. Here is the flow and the reasoning behind it:

1
Functional Overview — Orient before you configure
We start with a short, high-level answer to: what does this app do, who uses it, and why does it exist in the RPM suite? This isn't a deep dive — it's context-setting. You need to understand the shape of what you're about to build before you touch a single setting. 15 minutes max.
2
Sample Data Structures — Know the data before you configure it
Every configuration decision is a data decision in disguise. Before we open the configurator, we look at exactly what data the app expects — the objects, the required fields, the format, and the common problems. This section grounds you in what the customer needs to have ready before a configuration session can even begin. The CloudWorks dataset is introduced here.
3
Configurator Review — See every decision before you make it
The instructor walks through the full configurator page by page — live, with the app open. This is the Tell and Show. For each page you'll learn what the setting controls, what's at risk if you get it wrong, and what questions to ask the customer before answering it.
4
Lab: Configurator — Configure it yourself
Now you do it. Using the CloudWorks case study, you work through the configurator yourself — making the same decisions you just watched the instructor make, but for a specific customer scenario. Each step is a mini-lab with a clear objective, explicit actions, and a completion screenshot so you know whether you got it right. This is the most important part of each module. Don't skip it, don't rush it.
5
Post-Generation Steps — Generation is not go-live
The configurator triggers generation — but what comes out is not a customer-ready app. There is a defined sequence of post-generation work that must happen before you can put the app in front of a customer: fixing formula errors, remapping saved views, loading data, validating rollups, and confirming the core workflows function correctly. This section walks through that checklist so you know what to expect and who does what.
6
Model Review — Understand what was built
After generation, the app contains thousands of objects. You don't need to understand all of them — but you do need to understand the key structural patterns: which modules drive the core logic, how data flows through the model, and where to look first when something doesn't look right. The Model Review focuses on the handful of modules that matter most for implementation and troubleshooting.
7
Lab: CoModeler — Explore the model with AI assistance
CoModeler is Anaplan's AI-powered documentation tool. With the generated app open, you'll use it to produce a line item dictionary for a specific module, then ask targeted questions about formula logic — how does the quota calculation actually work? What module consolidates a rep's quota across multiple territory assignments? These aren't hypothetical questions — they're the exact questions that come up during implementation and customer conversations. The lab produces verifiable answers.
8
Application Review — Walk the app like a customer would
Now that you understand what was built and how it works under the hood, you walk the generated UX application end to end — the same way you would with a customer. Every section, every key page, every workflow. You'll learn where to spend your demo time, what to skip, and how to tell the planning story in business language rather than model builder language.
9
Common Extensions — Know when to build and when to configure
The last section of each module covers what customers consistently ask for beyond the base app — what it is, how to scope it, how long it takes, and how to build it in a way that doesn't break the upgrade path. Before scoping any extension, you'll use the configure-vs-extend decision tree to confirm the base app genuinely doesn't already handle the requirement. Often it does.
📝 T&Q module also includes

Data Integration & ADO — a dedicated section on how data flows into and out of the app, the V2 Data Hub vs. V3 ADO architecture, and what changes when your customer transitions to V3. This is covered in the T&Q module because T&Q is typically the first app implemented, and the data architecture decisions made here affect all three configurator apps.

📝 Sales Forecasting module

Sales Forecasting is covered as a single App Overview section rather than the full module structure above. It has no configurator, so there is no configurator lab or post-gen checklist — the app is deployed as-is with limited configuration options. The section covers capabilities, limitations, CRM integration, and how it connects to T&Q quota data.

The four modules covered in this workshop are:

🗺 Territory & Quota (T&Q)

The cornerstone of the RPM suite. Covers territory hierarchy design, account assignment, target allocation, quota calculation, and the approval workflow. The best landing app in the portfolio and the foundation for everything else.

🎯 Account Segmentation & Scoring

Classifies every account into a segment using rule-based and score-based logic. The output feeds directly into territory carving (T&Q) and headcount planning (Capacity). Run Segmentation before you design territories.

📊 Go-to-Market Capacity Planning

Answers "how many people do I need to hire, when, and where?" Combines finance-driven guardrails (tops-down) with tactical hiring plans (bottoms-up) connected through ramp profiles. Output informs the T&Q territory structure.

📈 Sales Forecasting

The newest addition to the RPM suite — now generally available. Structured forecast collection, pipeline analytics, and scenario modeling. Connects to CRM via ADO or Anaplan for Salesforce. Quota data flows in from T&Q.

What This Workshop Intends to Accomplish

By the end of this workshop, you should be able to do three things without hesitation:

1
Sit in a room with a customer and lead a configuration session — you know what questions to ask before each page, what settings to watch out for, and what decisions are hard to reverse. You're not following a script. You're making informed recommendations.
2
Demo the generated app in a customer meeting — you know every section of every app, what it does, who uses it, and where to focus for maximum impact in a 15–20 minute demo. You can answer follow-up questions without going back to the Apps team.
3
Scope an engagement — you can look at a customer's requirements and know whether the base app handles them, whether configuration addresses them, or whether a net-new extension is needed. You can estimate effort and explain the upgrade path implications.

How to Think About Completing This Workshop

💡 The Right Mindset

Don't come to this workshop to watch. Come to do. The demonstrations exist to prepare you for the labs — not to replace them. Every lab in this guide is designed to produce something real: a configuration decision, a working answer, a documented model structure. If you skip the labs, you're only half here.

Here is how to get the most out of each section:

Section TypeHow to Engage
Tell sections (overview, review) Read actively. Flag anything you don't understand. Write down questions — the debrief at the end of each lab is the place to ask them. Don't let ambiguity accumulate.
Show sections (facilitator demo) Open the app in your workspace and follow along. Don't just watch — navigate to the same page, see the same setting, confirm your understanding in real time. If something looks different in your workspace, say so.
Do sections (labs) Complete every step. Check your work against the completion screenshots. If your screen doesn't match, figure out why before moving on — the answer is usually a missed step or a setting from a prior page. This is where the learning happens.
Debrief questions Don't skip these. They're not filler — they're the bridge between executing steps and understanding why. If you can answer the debrief questions confidently, you understood the lab. If you can't, you executed it.

Why Lab Participation and Completion Matter

Every configuration lab in this workshop uses the CloudWorks Technologies case study — a consistent fictional customer that follows you through all three configurators. This is deliberate. By the time you finish the Capacity lab, you'll know CloudWorks well enough to answer edge-case questions without looking them up.

Labs Build Muscle Memory

Reading about the Data Hub Setup page is not the same as completing it. The configurator has a specific sequence, specific dependencies between pages, and specific ways to get it wrong. You need to feel that sequence — not just know it exists. The lab creates that familiarity.

Labs Surface Real Questions

The questions that matter most — the ones that come up in customer sessions — only surface when you're actually doing the work. "What if the CRM only has actuals at the rep level?" is a question that comes up in the lab, not from reading the walkthrough. That's the point.

CoModeler Labs Build Durable Knowledge

The model review sections teach you what to look for. The CoModeler labs force you to find it yourself. Generating a line item dictionary for the quota calculation module and reading it — that's something you'll remember. Being told about it is not.

Completion is a Signal

Each lab has a "what it should look like when complete" screenshot. If your screen matches, you're done. If it doesn't, you're not — regardless of how far through the steps you got. This is how production implementations work: the output has to match the spec.

🚫 Don't Generate During Labs

Do not run app generation against a customer workspace or a live workspace during this workshop. The configurator labs use a dedicated training workspace. Generating a T&Q app creates approximately 8,000 objects and cannot be easily undone. If you're unsure which workspace to use, ask the facilitator before clicking anything.

  • ✅ You can scope an extension request and explain the upgrade path risk

Build the habit of using it now so it's second nature in the field.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

  1. Position the full RPM suite — articulate all five SPM pillars, explain how each app fits the revenue planning lifecycle, and identify the strongest landing scenarios for each app.
  2. Configure T&Q, Segmentation, and Capacity — work through all three configurators with confidence, make informed decisions on each setting, and understand which choices are difficult to reverse.
  3. Execute post-generation steps — apply the post-gen checklist for each app, identify and resolve common issues, and assess model readiness for customer handoff.
  4. Demo every app end-to-end — guide a customer through T&Q, Segmentation, and Capacity in business terms, identify the key demo moments, and handle follow-up questions.
  5. Use CoModeler to document generated models — generate line item dictionaries, explain formula logic, and answer specific questions about model structure.
  6. Scope extensions cleanly — distinguish configure from extend, estimate effort, and build extensions in a way that survives upgrades.
  7. Explain Sales Forecasting — describe what the app does, how it connects to T&Q, its current limitations, and how to position it in a customer conversation.

How the Apps Connect

All four RPM apps are designed to work together. Understanding the data flows between them is essential context before you start any individual module.

⚠ Connections Are Not Automatic Today

Data flows between apps currently require a manual export/import step. Native app-to-app connections are on the roadmap. Account for this in your project timelines — it is a real implementation step, not a checkbox.

Segmentation → T&Q

Account segments from the Segmentation app are exported and imported as account attributes in T&Q. These drive territory carving, the Rule Engine, and addressable market calculations.

Segmentation → Capacity

Account counts per segment feed directly into the Capacity coverage ratio calculations. Changing segmentation changes required headcount — the two models are tightly linked.

Capacity → T&Q

Territories-per-headcount (TPH) output from Capacity informs how many territories to design in T&Q. The right sequencing is a judgment call — capacity planning and territory design often happen in parallel, each informing the other. What’s better defined at the start of the engagement should go first.

T&Q → Sales Forecasting

Quotas set in T&Q flow into Sales Forecasting as the performance benchmark. Clean T&Q quota data is a prerequisite for meaningful forecast attainment tracking.

Recommended Implementation Sequence

The order below reflects common practice, but Segmentation, Capacity, and T&Q often run in parallel — each informs the others. Use your judgment based on what’s best-defined for the customer at project start.

  1. Segmentation — classify your account universe before designing territories
  2. Capacity Planning & T&Q — these often happen in parallel; headcount planning and territory design inform each other
  3. Sales Forecasting — add forecast tracking once quotas are live and trusted
