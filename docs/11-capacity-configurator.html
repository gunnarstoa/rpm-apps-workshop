⚠ Critical Dimensionality Warning

Dimensionality decisions in the Capacity configurator are extremely difficult to change post-generation. If you configure cost assumptions at the region level and later decide you need sub-region, you will need to disconnect and reconnect many formulaic references — or regenerate and lose any custom work.

Be thorough and deliberate with your customer. Validate every dimensionality choice before generating. This is the #1 source of rework on Capacity implementations.

🚨 Step 0 — Role Grade: Critical Decision

Step 0 on this page is where Role Grade is enabled or turned off. This is one of the most consequential decisions in the entire Capacity configurator.

Role Grade is an optional additional dimension (e.g., grade levels P1–P5 within a Sales Role). Enabling it adds substantial complexity — every coverage, productivity, and hiring table gains an additional dimension for every grade level. This multiplies the number of planning rows across the entire app.

Only enable Role Grade if the customer genuinely needs grade-level headcount planning and has the data to support it. Challenge this requirement rigorously with the customer before enabling. If Role Grade is enabled when it isn't needed, significant re-work is required to remove it post-generation.

Page 1.2.2 configures the guardrail structure for finance-driven headcount planning — the tops-down Not-to-Exceed (NTE) ceiling that constrains all bottoms-up plans submitted at lower levels.

Key tables and configuration decisions (verify Step labels in live UX):

  • Step 0 — Role Grade — Enable or disable role grade dimension. See critical callout above.
  • Sales role / job family hierarchy — define the structure of the sales role hierarchy: up to 3 levels (Job Family Group → Job Family → Sales Role). How many levels do you actually need? More levels = more granular planning and more maintenance overhead.
  • Tops-down planning level — at what level of the geo hierarchy does tops-down target setting begin? (Usually the top — all Americas, or global). This sets the NTE guardrails that constrain bottoms-up planning at every level below.
  • Scenario planning — how many planning scenarios does the customer want to model? (Typically 2–3: conservative, base, optimistic).

Page 1.2.3 defines how roles cover accounts — the fundamental input for calculating how many people are needed. This is where the customer's coverage model is translated into the planning framework.

Key tables and configuration decisions (verify Step labels in live UX):

  • Geography level for coverage assumptions — configure at what level of geography coverage assumptions are managed (e.g., all Americas at once, or per sub-region). More granular = more flexibility but more maintenance.
  • Direct seller coverage (account-based) — 1 AE per N accounts. Configure the ratio per role and per account segment (e.g., Strategic AE → 1:5 accounts covering Strategic + Named segments).
  • Overlay / support role coverage (derived) — 1 generalist per N AEs, 1 manager per N AEs. Coverage is derived from the direct seller headcount rather than directly from account counts.
  • Segment mapping — which roles cover which account segments (e.g., Strategic AEs cover Strategic + Named accounts only). This is the direct connection point to Segmentation app outputs.
  • Hours-based planning — optional workload model for non-revenue roles (e.g., 1 specialist handles 32 hours/week of customer requests). Rarely used — revenue-based coverage is the standard approach.

Page 1.2.4 configures the tactical hiring planning layer — where planners actually enter month-by-month hire plans. This is the bottoms-up counterpart to the tops-down NTE set in 1.2.2.

Key tables and configuration decisions (verify Step labels in live UX):

  • Bottoms-up entry level — at what level are bottoms-up hiring plans entered? (Typically territory or leaf level of the geo hierarchy.)
  • Approval levels — once submitted, at what level does the plan get approved? (e.g., submitted at sub-region, approved at region). The approved plan cannot exceed the NTE from tops-down.
  • OWP connection — optional import of data from Anaplan Workforce Planning (OWP) — ramping profiles, historical headcount data. Define at what level this data comes in if the customer uses OWP. Leave disabled if not applicable.
