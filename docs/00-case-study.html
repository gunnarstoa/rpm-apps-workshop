    Case Study: CloudWorks Technologies

    The fictional company used as the lab scenario throughout this workshop

    Getting Started Reference

    Every lab exercise in this workshop uses CloudWorks Technologies as the customer scenario. Using a consistent fictional company across all modules means the data, decisions, and context carry forward — by the time you finish the Capacity lab, you'll know CloudWorks well enough to answer edge-case questions without looking them up.

    💡 Keep This Page Open

    Refer back here whenever a lab asks you to make a decision based on the customer's situation. The company profile, hierarchy, and data details here are the source of truth for all CloudWorks configuration decisions.

    Company Overview

    About CloudWorks

    • Type: Fictional B2B SaaS company
    • Products: Cloud infrastructure and developer tools
    • Markets: Mid-Market (<1,000 employees) and Enterprise (1,000+ employees)
    • Geography: North America and EMEA
    • CRM: Salesforce
    • Current State: Managing territory planning and quota-setting in Excel — moving to Anaplan RPM Apps

    Sales Organization

    • Sales Reps (AEs): 180 across NA and EMEA
    • Overlay Reps: 25 Pre-Sales Engineers (SEs)
    • Hierarchy: 5-tier territory structure
    • Fiscal Year: February – January (FY starts Feb 1)
    • Planning Granularity: Quarterly
    • Historical Actuals: 2 years (FY23, FY24)

    Territory Hierarchy

    CloudWorks uses a 5-tier territory hierarchy. This is the structure you'll configure in the T&Q Hierarchy Setup page:

    TierNameExample Values
    Tier 1GeoCloudWorks Global
    Tier 2AreaNA, EMEA
    Tier 3RegionUS-West, US-East, UK, DACH
    Tier 4Sub-RegionPacific NW, Mid-Atlantic, Benelux
    Tier 5TerritoryIndividual rep territories (~220 total)

    Planning Measures

    MeasureTypeNotes
    ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)Currency (USD)Primary planning measure — all targets and quotas are set against ARR
    New LogosCount (Integer)Secondary measure — tracks net-new customer acquisition

    Data & Integration

    Data Sources

    • CRM: Salesforce — source of actuals and account data
    • Actuals level: Account level (Account ID + Quarter + ARR)
    • Data Hub: New — no existing Anaplan for SPM
    • Integration: ADO (IT team confirmed readiness)

    Finance & Targets

    • Target entry level: Area (Finance provides targets at the NA / EMEA level)
    • Allocation methods: Weighted by Historical Actuals + Manual Override
    • Over-allocation: Enabled — max 115%
    • Approval workflow: 2-stage — Regional Manager → Area VP

    Account Assignment

    Assignment Methods

    • Manual Assignment: Enabled — used for named enterprise accounts
    • Geographic Mapping: Disabled — territories are not geo-defined
    • Rule Engine: Enabled — used for mid-market accounts
    • Optimizer: Disabled — not in scope for this implementation
    • Split Accounts: Enabled — enterprise accounts can span territories

    Rule Engine Attributes

    The Rule Engine uses these account attributes for assignment logic:

    • Industry (list)
    • Account Segment (Strategic / Named / Commercial / SMB)
    • Employee Count (banded)

    Example rule: Industry = "Technology" AND Segment = "Commercial" → US-West Commercial Territory

    Segmentation Profile

    CloudWorks uses four account segments. These are defined during the Segmentation configurator exercise:

    SegmentCriteria (Rule-Based)AE Coverage Ratio
    StrategicHigh wallet tier + enterprise industry + revenue band >$100M1 AE : 5 accounts
    NamedLarge employee count + key target industry (below Strategic threshold)1 AE : 5 accounts (shared with Strategic)
    CommercialMid-size accounts — employee count 250–1,0001 AE : 30 accounts
    SMBSmaller accounts — employee count <2501 AE : 100 accounts

    Score-based segmentation uses three weighted metrics: Employee Band (40%), Revenue Band (35%), Industry (25%). Score thresholds: 7–10 = Strategic, 5–7 = Named, 3–5 = Commercial, 0–3 = SMB.

    Capacity Planning Profile

    Role Hierarchy

    • Job Family Groups: Core, Support
    • Job Families: AE, CS, Pre-Sales, Management
    • Sales Roles: Strategic AE, Commercial AE, SMB AE, Customer Success Manager, Pre-Sales Engineer, Regional Manager
    • Role Grade: Not used — 3-level hierarchy only

    Planning Setup

    • Revenue targets: Provided at sub-region level by account segment
    • Tops-down level: Americas
    • Scenarios: Base + Conservative
    • Bottoms-up entry: Territory level
    • Approval level: Region
    • Overlay coverage: 1 generalist per 8 AEs; 1 manager per 10 AEs

    Lab Dataset Files

    The following files are provided in the exercise zip and used in the T&Q configurator lab. Distributed via the shared drive folder before the workshop.

    FileRowsKey Fields
    Configurator Exercise Account_Flat File.csv~4,200Account ID, Name, Industry, Segment, Employee Count, Annual Revenue, Territory, Lat/Long, ARR Spend
    Configurator Exercise Territory_Flat File.csv~220Territory ID, Territory Name, Territory Parent, Territory Manager
    Configurator Exercise Employee File.xlsx~205Employee ID, Employee Name, Sales Role, Job Family, Hire Date, Source Territory, Manager
    Configurator Exercise Opportunity line File.csv~18,000Opportunity Line ID, Opportunity ID (parent), Account, Amount, Planning Measure, Service Date
    Opportunity (parent record)—The Opportunity record is the parent of Opportunity Line. Depending on how the customer’s CRM exports data, both files may be required. Opportunity carries header-level fields (close date, stage, owner) while Opportunity Line carries the line-item amounts. Confirm with the customer whether their actuals export is at the Opportunity or Opportunity Line level.

    Your Role in the Labs

    In every lab exercise, you are playing the role of the Anaplan delivery lead on the CloudWorks implementation. CloudWorks has completed Session Zero and provided sample data. The stakeholders have signed off on the key decisions listed on this page. Your job is to translate those decisions into the correct configurator settings — and to catch anything that doesn't add up.

    ✅ Do — Before Starting the Labs

    Spend 5 minutes reading this page before your first configurator lab. Then, as you work through each lab, refer back here whenever you need to make a decision. Ask yourself: "Would this setting make sense for CloudWorks based on what I know about them?" That's the right instinct to develop.
